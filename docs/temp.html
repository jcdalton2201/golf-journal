Along the Pacific coast is the modern-day mecca for golfers. Bandon Dunes is the west coast version of Pinehurst. With five courses, a par 3, and a punch bowl putting course, it has set how golf resorts should be. The first course for us was Pacific Dunes. Pacific was the second course built on the property. The more dramatic layout, the Pacific course has more towering dunes, more significant undulations, and tricker greens then Bandon. Still, it gives you plenty of opportunities to make strategic decisions during your round.
You will forget that you are still in the states as you play this course. Visions of Scotland and Ireland will infiltrate your every senses. The links-style of play is here in spades. If you like that style, you will love this course. Travel here is not for the faint of heart. It is a four-hour drive from Portland unless you are lucky to get a seasonal flight into North Bend OR 43 miles north of Bandon. The other bonus of Bandon Dunes is the replay rate. If you are one that likes to play 36 a day, then it is very doable. I would recommend a caddie on these courses. They can give you valuable information on how to play the course.
